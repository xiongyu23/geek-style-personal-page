Research

Our research program bridges pure mathematics and practical financial applications, focusing on dynamical systems, symbolic dynamics, point process theory, stochastic optimal control, and computational methods applied to market microstructure and high-frequency trading.

Primary Research Domains

Order Book Dynamics (LOB) Modeling

We apply dynamical systems theory to model the complex behavior of limit order books, developing mathematical frameworks to describe price formation, liquidity evolution, and order flow dynamics. Our approach leverages symbolic dynamics to capture the structural patterns in order book events.

Key methodologies: Hawkes processes, point process theory, symbolic dynamics, stochastic differential equations

High-Frequency Market Microstructure

Research into the fine-grained structure of financial markets, focusing on how order flow, liquidity provision, and trading behavior interact at millisecond and microsecond time scales. We develop novel metrics for market quality and price impact assessment.

Key focus: Micro-price discovery, order flow imbalance, liquidity taker/maker dynamics, market impact modeling

Computational Finance & Machine Learning

Integration of machine learning architectures with traditional quantitative finance models. We develop hybrid models that combine symbolic dynamics insights with deep learning techniques for enhanced market prediction and strategy optimization.

Key focus: Neural ODEs, reinforcement learning, explainable AI, hybrid symbolic-statistical models

Low-Latency Algorithm Design

Research into the mathematical optimization of trading algorithms for ultra-low latency execution. We combine numerical analysis with high-performance computing to develop algorithms that maintain mathematical rigor while achieving nanosecond-level execution times.

Key focus: Numerical optimization, parallel computing, Rust performance engineering, cache-efficient algorithms

Adaptive Risk Management

Development of dynamic risk assessment frameworks based on stochastic control theory and dynamical systems. Our risk models adapt to changing market conditions while maintaining rigorous mathematical guarantees on portfolio stability.

Key focus: Stochastic optimal control, robust portfolio optimization, real-time risk bounds, drawdown control

Research Methodology

Our research process combines rigorous mathematical derivation with empirical validation, following a structured approach that ensures both theoretical soundness and practical applicability:

01

Theoretical Development

Derivation of mathematical models based on dynamical systems, point process theory, stochastic optimal control, and computational methods, with rigorous proofs of convergence and stability properties.

02

Numerical Implementation

High-performance numerical implementation of theoretical models using Rust and C++ for computational efficiency.

03

Empirical Validation

Rigorous testing against historical market data, with focus on out-of-sample performance and robustness across market regimes.

04

Implementation Optimization

Refinement of models for real-world deployment, including latency optimization and integration with trading infrastructure.

Research Applications

Trading Signal Generation

Our research translates into actionable trading signals based on LOB dynamics, order flow imbalance, and micro-price prediction, all derived from our mathematical models of market behavior.

Execution Algorithm Design

Low-latency execution algorithms optimized using our research into market impact and liquidity dynamics, minimizing transaction costs for large order execution.

Risk Assessment Tools

Real-time risk monitoring systems that leverage our dynamical systems models to identify emerging risk factors before they impact portfolio performance.

API-Based Signal Delivery

Our upcoming API platform will make our research-derived signals available to institutional clients, with documentation of the mathematical foundations behind each signal type.
